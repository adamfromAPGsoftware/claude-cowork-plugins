## Section 9: Key Patterns & Takeaways

1. **High-Energy, Jump-Cut Intro:** Opens with 39 seconds of full-frame speaker, utilizing jump-cut zooms (~1.05x) every 3-5 seconds to artificially boost visual pacing and remove all dead air.
2. **Static PiP Configuration:** PiP speaker is positioned bottom-left, rounded rectangle shape (~18% frame width), with no visible border, present during 100% of screen share time.
3. **Live Annotation over Post-Production:** Instead of editing in zooms or highlights, the creator uses a live digital drawing tool during screen shares to guide the viewer's eye and maintain visual motion.
4. **The "Speaker Sandwich" Transition:** Major section changes follow a strict pattern: Screen Share → Full-Frame Speaker (for transition statement) → Title Card → New Screen Share.
5. **Zero-Fluff Editing:** 100% hard cuts. No cross-dissolves, no slide transitions, no sound effects, and no background music.
6. **Long Screen Share Holds:** Once in a screen share layout, the camera holds for minutes at a time without cutting, relying entirely on the UI interaction and voiceover to carry the segment.
7. **Minimalist Typography:** Text is restricted to simple black-and-white title cards. No lower thirds or pop-up text graphics are used.
8. **Missing Captions:** No burned-in captions are present, representing an area for improvement in automated recreation.
