## S1: Video Lead Magnet

**Source posts:** *(populate with your top-performing video post IDs)*

**When to use:** When there's a YouTube video with a strong demo moment, or when the topic can be proven visually (tool running, code building, install completing). The video IS the argument.

**Best hook types:**
- Gap/Contrarian: "Every [X] does [Y]. None of them [achieve the thing that matters]."
- Provocative claim: "[Skill/tool/approach] is the next [thing you won't need]."
- Number anchor: "[Minimal input]. [Massive output]. Watch how fast this [does the thing]."

### Template Skeleton

```
[Hook — one of the 3 hook types above, 1-2 lines max]

[Credibility/origin line — why you can make this claim, 1 line]

[What you're watching / what you built — 1-2 lines, specific stack or tool]

[Not X. Y. — contrast what this ISN'T vs what it IS]

What you're watching:  [OR: A real system:]
→ [specific thing 1]
→ [specific thing 2]
→ [specific thing 3]

[Credibility anchor — "Built from / After {YOUR_PROJECT_COUNT}+ projects"]

Want [specific resource]?
1. Like this post
2. Comment "[KEYWORD]"
3. Connect with me so I can DM you

P.S. — [Specific promise or honest qualifier for the reader it's designed for]
```

**Tone rules:**
- Contractions mandatory: "I've", "that's", "you're"
- Fragment sentences in hook are fine: "One command. 60,000 skills."
- Use dry rhetorical devices when an unexpected result appears
- No em dashes in the body (hook exception allowed for the ironic observation beat)

**Visual guidance:**
- Source a 15-30s segment from a relevant YouTube video — screen recording preferred
- Pick the moment where something builds or a result appears (not talking heads)
- ffmpeg: `ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -ss {start} -t {duration} -vf "setpts=0.05*PTS" -an -c:v libx264 output-20x.mp4`
- For install/terminal videos: 2-5x speed is enough to be impressive, 20x for build sequences

**Character target:** 900-1200 chars

## S2: Carousel Lead Magnet

**Source post:** *(populate with your top-performing carousel post ID)*

**When to use:** When the topic has 6-10 distinct components that each deserve their own slide. Works best when the carousel itself can be a meta-proof of the process (i.e., the carousel was created by the AI system being described).

**Best hook types:**
- Result + Honest Qualifier: "[Specific outcome]. Not by [the obvious/fake method]. By [the real mechanism]."
- Process reveal: "I [built/created/automated] [specific thing] that [transformed metric from X to Y]."

### Template Skeleton

```
[Result claim — specific transformation, 1 line]

[Anti-qualifier — "Not by [cheap/obvious approach]"]

[Real mechanism — "By [the actual thing that no one else is doing]"]

[Bridge — "The [thing] handles [the specific boring work] that eats [%] of [person's] time"]

[List title line:]
→ [component 1]
→ [component 2]
→ [component 3]
→ [component 4]
→ [component 5]
→ [component 6]

[Authenticity defence — "Everything you see is still me. Real voice, real [X], real [Y]."]

[What the AI actually does — specific actions, not vague "AI handles it"]

Want [specific resource]?
1. Like this post
2. Comment "[KEYWORD]"
3. Connect with me so I can DM you

P.S. — [Meta-proof: "This carousel was generated by one of the [things being described]"]
```

**Tone rules:**
- The anti-qualifier ("Not by generating fake AI content") is essential — kills skepticism before it forms
- Authenticity defence is mandatory for AI-generated content posts
- P.S. should be meta-proof whenever possible — the format IS the demo

**Visual guidance:**
- Generate carousel via your carousel generation workflow
- Each → list item becomes its own slide
- Dark background, brand accent colour
- 6-8 slides max, one idea per slide

**Character target:** 700-900 chars (post text), carousel slides have their own content

## S3: Authority Contrarian

**Source post:** *(populate with your top-performing contrarian post ID)*

**When to use:** When there's a data/math proof that exposes a flaw in the conventional approach. The hook must name the person being replaced/disrupted and add an ironic detail about what they're still doing.

**Best hook:**
- Industry Alarm + Ironic Detail: "Most [audience] are about to be [disrupted] — and they're still [doing the outdated thing]."

### Template Skeleton

```
[Hook — "Most [audience] are about to be [replaced/disrupted] — and they're still [outdated behaviour]"]

Here's the problem nobody's talking about:

[Math proof or specific stat that shows the flaw in the current approach]
[e.g., "0.8^5 = 0.33" or "$X/month for tools that don't talk to each other"]

That's not [dismissal word]. That's a broken system.

→ [Platform/tool] [does the thing that's broken]
→ [You can't fix it without doing X]
→ [It gets worse as it scales]
→ [And you're paying [amount] for it]

I [moved/replaced/rebuilt] my entire [stack/system/approach] — [{YOUR_PROJECT_COUNT}+ projects] — [last month/recently].

[3-word contrast to what they're replacing]. [3-word contrast]. [3-word contrast].

Instead of [old behaviour], I [new behaviour]. [Benefit of that behaviour].

[Comparative math or stat that shows the improvement]

That's not incremental. That's a different game.

[What the solution is — open-source, free, one-line install, etc.]

Comment "[KEYWORD]" and I'll send you [specific resource].

P.S. — [Bridge to existing tool users: "If you've already [done the thing], you already have the mental model."]
```

**Tone rules:**
- The ironic observation in the hook must be specific enough to identify the exact target reader
- Math as argument: if you can show the compounding accuracy problem as an equation, do it
- "That's not [word]. That's [reframe]." — verdict pattern appears twice (after math, after comparison)
- Three-word parallel sentences as a contrast device
- Single-line CTA, not 3-step — the post is already dense, 3-step feels padded

**Visual guidance:** Text only. The math/argument IS the visual. No image.

**Character target:** 1000-1300 chars

## S4: Personal Origin Story

**Source post:** *(populate with your best personal story post ID)*

**When to use:** Personal pillar only. When there's a genuine turning point moment in your journey that teaches something relevant to the audience's current situation. Must have a specific, quotable thesis at the end.

**Best hook:**
- Achievement Claim + Deflation: "[Impressive milestone]. Not [what reader assumes]. [Smaller/harder truth]."

### Template Skeleton

```
[Hook — claim + deflation, 2-3 lines]

[Scene detail — where, when, what circumstances — specific physical details]

[What was missing — "No [thing 1]. No [thing 2]. No [thing 3] telling me what to do."]

[Tension line — "Most people would've quit. I almost did."]

But here's what nobody tells you about [the hard thing]:
→ Every [failure] taught me [lesson]
→ Every [small thing] taught me [lesson]
→ Every [difficult thing] taught me [lesson]

[Time jump — "X years later, [starting point] turned into [outcome]."]

[Contrarian observation about the people who "made it fast"]

[Thesis as a one-liner — "The long way is actually the shortcut."]

If you're in [month/year/stage] and wondering if it's worth it — it is.

But only if [the real condition for success — not a generic platitude].
```

**Tone rules:**
- NO CTA. Ever. This is pure resonance. Adding a keyword ask would destroy the authenticity.
- Specific physical details beat abstract descriptions ("kitchen counter at night" > "early in my career")
- Short declarative sentences for the "No X. No Y." section — stacked negatives build atmosphere
- Thesis must be a single quotable line, not a paragraph
- End with direct address to the reader, but no question and no ask

**Visual guidance:** Text only. The specificity of the language IS the image. No photo needed.

**Character target:** 700-900 chars

## S5: Personal Event / Authority Signal

**Source post:** *(populate with your best event/speaking post ID)*

**When to use:** After attending an event, speaking engagement, or any moment where you were in a room with impressive people. The hook establishes the event and quantifies the room, then pivots to what you observed — not self-congratulation.

**Best hook:**
- Scene-setting Status Signal: "I just [did specific thing] at [specific event] to [specific audience with quantified reach/credential]."

### Template Skeleton

```
[Hook — "I just [spoke/presented/attended] at [Event] to [description of audience + quantified reach/credential]"]

[Room description — who was there, the calibre]

[What others were doing (brief) — creates contrast for your contribution]

[Your angle — "And there I was talking about the thing I know best →"]

[Biggest takeaway — "My biggest takeaway from the event →"]

[Pain insight or observation from conversations — ideally a direct quote from someone]

[Your proof that this is not new — "That's been the story for [{YOUR_PROJECT_COUNT}+] of our projects"]

[Authentic close — gratitude + energy statement, no CTA]

[Final line — elevates the space, not you]
```

**Tone rules:**
- Self-deprecating entry into your contribution: "And there I was talking about the thing I know best →"
- The event is the story, not you — you're a character in it, not the hero
- Close with genuine emotion ("more fired up than I've been in a long time") — not "excited to share"
- Final line should be about the people in the space, not your achievement
- NO CTA, NO open question, NO "let me know what you think"

**Visual guidance:**
- Real photo of you at the event — stage photo, candid shot, or outdoor event photo
- Crop to 1:1 for LinkedIn feed
- Real event photo > AI-generated for this style — authenticity is the whole point

**Character target:** 600-800 chars

## Cross-Style DNA: What All Posts Share

Regardless of style, every post should have these characteristics:

1. **Project count anchor** — appears as a single-line credibility anchor (e.g., "250+ projects")
2. **→ arrows for lists** — never bullets, never numbered for descriptive lists (numbered only for CTAs)
3. **No hashtags** — ever, anywhere
4. **P.S. lines** — present in all lead magnet posts, never in personal posts
5. **Specific numbers** — always numerals, always specific (30h not "many hours", $1,000 not "some money")
6. **Short paragraphs** — single-line paragraphs with line breaks between every section
7. **Contractions** — "I've", "that's", "you're", "it's" — always
8. **No open questions as closers** — posts end with statements or direct address, never "What do you think?"
9. **Anti-hype honesty** — include an honest caveat, an unexpected vulnerability, or an "Honest? Yes." moment
10. **Credibility is woven in** — authority comes from specifics (project counts, % time saved, equations), not self-declaration
