## Weekly Frequency Targets

**Important:** "Max/Week" is the point of diminishing returns, not a hard cap. You *can* post more — especially on Instagram Reels and TikTok — but quality per post matters more than volume. The algorithm rewards watch time and engagement rate, not posting frequency.

| Platform | Content Type | Min/Week | Sweet Spot | Max/Week | Daily Cap | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Reels | 3 | 5 | 7 | 2/day | Mosseri recommends 3-7/week; each Reel needs time to breathe for algorithm distribution |
| Instagram | Feed (static/carousel) | 2 | 3 | 5 | 1/day | Carousels get ~10% avg engagement; complement Reels, don't replace them |
| Instagram | Stories | 7 | 14 | 35 | 5/day | Low-effort, keeps profile active, doesn't compete with feed algorithm |
| YouTube | Long-form | 1 | 1 | 2 | — | 2x/week grows 3x faster than 1x; quality is the bottleneck |
| YouTube | Shorts | 3 | 5 | 7 | 1-2/day | Takes 200+ uploads before consistent lift; consistency > volume |
| TikTok | Videos | 2 | 3 | 5 | 1-2/day | Official rec is 1-4/day but over-posting causes content cannibalization |
| LinkedIn | Posts | 2 | 3 | 5 | 1/day | Carousels get 278% more engagement than video; avoid weekends |

## Spacing Rules

Minimum time between posts on the same platform to avoid algorithm suppression and content cannibalization.

| Platform | Min Hours Between Posts | Same-Hour Prohibition | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram (Reels) | 4 hours | Yes | Algorithm needs time to distribute each Reel |
| Instagram (Feed) | 6 hours | Yes | Feed posts compete with each other |
| Instagram (Stories) | 1 hour | No | Stories are sequential, not competing |
| YouTube (Long-form) | 48 hours | Yes | Give each video 48h of initial algorithm push |
| YouTube (Shorts) | 4 hours | Yes | Shorts shelf has limited slots per creator |
| TikTok | 3 hours | Yes | FYP distribution window per video |
| LinkedIn | 18 hours | Yes | LinkedIn throttles rapid consecutive posts |

---

## Cross-Platform Timing Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Stagger cross-posts** | When posting the same content across platforms, stagger by 1-2 hours minimum |
| **Daily post cap** | Maximum 5 posts across ALL platforms in a single day (excluding Stories) |
| **Platform priority** | Post to the primary platform first, then adapt and cross-post |
| **Same-content gap** | If adapting the same content for multiple platforms, space by at least 2 hours |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Weekend LinkedIn posting | Wasted reach | 60%+ engagement drop on Sat/Sun; save posts for Tue-Thu |
| Content cannibalization | Suppressed reach | Posting 3+ times/day on Instagram or TikTok splits your audience across posts |
| Batch-dumping | Algorithm penalty | Scheduling 5+ posts within a 2-hour window across platforms signals automation |
| Ignoring time zones | Missed audience | AEST times assume AU/APAC audience; adjust if targeting US/EU |
| Same time every day | Predictability decay | Vary posting times by 30-60 min to reach different audience segments |
| Posting long-form YouTube on weekdays after 6 PM | Low initial velocity | Most viewers discover via browse/suggested, which favours daytime uploads |
| LinkedIn posts with external links in body | ~40-50% reach suppression | Always put links in first comment |
| Over-hashtagging on LinkedIn | Spam signal | 3-5 hashtags max; more triggers spam filters |

## How to Use This Guide

When recommending a time slot:

1. **Check the Best Times table** for the target platform and find the strongest available window
2. **Check the content calendar** for existing posts on that platform in the same week
3. **Apply Spacing Rules** — ensure minimum hours between posts on the same platform
4. **Apply Cross-Platform Rules** — stagger if other platforms have posts near the same time
5. **Check Weekly Frequency** — compare current week's count against the frequency table
6. **Avoid Anti-Patterns** — verify the suggestion doesn't match any anti-pattern
7. **Present 3 ranked options** with reasons, letting the user choose
