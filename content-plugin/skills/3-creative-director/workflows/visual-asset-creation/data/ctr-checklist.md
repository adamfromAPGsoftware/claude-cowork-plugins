# CTR Validation Checklist

## 7-Point Validation

Run this checklist against every generated thumbnail. Score each check as PASS, WARN, or FAIL.

| # | Check | PASS | WARN | FAIL |
|---|-------|------|------|------|
| 1 | Text character count | <12 chars | 12-15 chars | >15 chars |
| 2 | 3-element composition | All 3 present (face + object/context + text) | 2 of 3 | 1 or 0 |
| 3 | Curiosity gap | Title + thumbnail create information gap | Weak gap | No gap |
| 4 | Title-thumbnail coherence | Complementary, single unit | Loosely related | Redundant or disconnected |
| 5 | Mobile readability | Clear at ~168x94px phone size | Legible with effort | Unreadable |
| 6 | Emotional trigger | Expression matches content tone | Neutral expression | Wrong emotion |
| 7 | ICP relevance | Both ICPs targeted | Primary ICP only | Neither ICP |

## Key Data Points

- Videos with human faces get 921K more views than without
- Sad/shocked expressions: highest avg views (2.3M) despite only 1.8% usage
- Under 12 characters of text significantly outperforms text-heavy designs
- 0.2 seconds: average viewer decision time on a thumbnail
- CTR benchmarks: Elite >10%, Strong 5-10%, Average 2-5%
- 80% of YouTube browsing is mobile — thumbnail displays at ~168x94px on phone

## The 3-Element Rule

Every high-performing thumbnail has exactly 3 elements:
1. **Face** — human face with clear emotional expression (left or right third)
2. **Object/Context** — visual element that grounds the topic (centre or background)
3. **Text** — short, punchy text overlay that complements the title (opposite third from face)

## Expression Performance Ranking

1. **Shocked/Surprised** — highest CTR, creates instant curiosity
2. **Curious/Intrigued** — second highest, invites viewer to learn
3. **Excited/Happy** — third, conveys positive energy
4. **Serious/Focused** — lowest CTR but appropriate for authority content

## Curiosity Gap Mechanics

- **Number + claim:** "3 MINUTES" (how? what happens?)
- **Before/after implied:** show transformation, title explains how
- **Contradiction:** visual contradicts expectation, title resolves
- **Missing piece:** thumbnail shows result, title teases the method

## Colour Psychology

- **Red/Orange** — urgency, excitement, energy
- **Blue** — trust, authority, calm
- **Yellow/Green** — growth, money, positivity
- **High contrast** outperforms monochrome every time

## Text Rules

- Under 12 characters — mandatory for mobile readability
- Complement, don't duplicate the title
- High contrast — must pop at phone-screen size
- Bold, simple fonts — no thin/script fonts
- One line max
