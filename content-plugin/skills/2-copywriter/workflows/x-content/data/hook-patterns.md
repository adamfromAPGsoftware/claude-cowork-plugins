# X/Twitter Hook Patterns

Hook archetypes, format-specific guidance, and quality rules for X content.

---

## What Makes an X Hook Work

X hooks face a harder context than LinkedIn:
- Feed moves faster — reader decision is sub-second
- "Show more" cutoff at ~140 chars on mobile — hook must land inside this window
- Tone expectation: direct, confident, slightly raw — not polished corporate
- Single posts need a hook that works standalone
- Thread tweet 1 needs a hook that earns the "read more" click AND signals there's a sequence

**Hook quality rules:**
1. Specific > vague ("4 hours" > "a lot of time")
2. Confident > hedged ("This is wrong" > "I think this might be wrong")
3. Creates forward pull — reader needs to know what comes next
4. Targets a real pain point or aspiration of the ICP
5. Not clickbait — the hook must be delivered on in the body
6. Does NOT duplicate hooks already used for this project/topic (check derivative tracking)

## Hook Archetypes

### 1. Contrarian
Challenges a commonly held belief. High engagement because it triggers "wait, what?" response.

**Template:**
```
[Common belief] is [wrong/a myth/killing your X].
Here's what actually [happens/works]:
```

**X-specific notes:**
- Name the belief specifically — vague contrarianism gets ignored
- Must deliver the counter-argument in the body — can't just be provocative
- Works best for: personal posts, hot takes, nurture/educational content

**Example hooks:**
- "Posting daily is terrible advice for most creators. Here's why:"
- "More followers don't mean more revenue. I learned this at 50K."

---

### 2. Bold Claim
States a strong outcome or conclusion. Credibility must follow in the body.

**Template:**
```
[I/We] [achieved X] [in timeframe/with constraint].
[Here's how / Thread:]
```

**X-specific notes:**
- Numbers make it specific and credible
- The claim must be true and verifiable — X readers call out exaggeration fast
- Works best for: threads, lead magnets, authority-building

**Example hooks:**
- "I cut my meeting load by 70% without losing any stakeholder trust. Thread on how:"
- "Built a working AI agent in 45 minutes. No prior ML experience needed."

---

### 3. Curiosity Gap
Creates an information gap the reader is compelled to close.

**Template:**
```
Nobody talks about [hidden cost / real reason / overlooked thing] of [popular thing].
[Let me fix that. / Thread:]
```

**X-specific notes:**
- The gap must feel genuine — not manufactured
- Deliver the reveal clearly in the first 2-3 body tweets/paragraphs
- Works best for: threads, long posts, nurture content

**Example hooks:**
- "Nobody talks about the real cost of building in public. I've been doing it for 2 years."
- "There's a step missing from every 'how to build a SaaS' guide. I found it the hard way."

---

### 4. Number-Led
Opens with a specific number — the brain parses numbers faster than prose.

**Template:**
```
[N] [things/lessons/mistakes/tools] that [outcome]:
```

**X-specific notes:**
- Odd numbers feel more authentic than round numbers (7 > 10)
- Smaller counts work better for single posts; larger for threads
- Works best for: threads (body = numbered list), single posts (if tight enough)

**Example hooks:**
- "5 Claude prompts I use every single day:"
- "3 mistakes that made my first product launch fail."

---

### 5. Story Opener
Opens in a scene or moment. Creates narrative momentum.

**Template:**
```
[Month, Year]. [Dramatic/pivotal moment in one sentence.]
[What happened next / what I did next / thread:]
```

**X-specific notes:**
- Drop the reader in media res — don't build up to the moment
- The scene must be specific: time, place, or clear context
- Works best for: personal posts, story threads, long posts

**Example hooks:**
- "March 2024. My SaaS had 0 users and I'd spent $12K building it."
- "My co-founder quit on a Tuesday morning. We had a launch on Friday."

---

### 6. How-To Promise
Explicit value delivery. Reader knows exactly what they get.

**Template:**
```
How to [achieve outcome] [without painful thing / in timeframe]:
```

**X-specific notes:**
- Must be specific — "How to grow on X" is too vague; "How to get your first 1K followers in 60 days" works
- The steps must be genuinely useful — not padded
- Works best for: threads, long posts, lead magnets

**Example hooks:**
- "How to write a thread that gets 1,000+ RTs (not luck — a system):"
- "How to get your first 10 B2B customers without cold email:"

---

### 7. Hot Take
Strong opinion designed to spark replies and engagement. Must be specific, not vague.

**Template:**
```
[Tool/Practice/Belief] is [overrated/dead/a mistake].
[Here's what I use instead / Here's why:]
```

**X-specific notes:**
- Vague hot takes die. Specific hot takes spread.
- Be willing to defend the take in the replies — X rewards conviction
- Works best for: single posts, personal/opinion content

**Example hooks:**
- "Notion is terrible for project management. Fight me."
- "AI-generated content sounds exactly like AI-generated content. Readers know."

## Format-Specific Hook Guidance

### Single Post
Hook must carry the entire post. No visual support. Must work in ≤140 chars (show more cutoff).
- Prefer: bold claims, hot takes, number-led
- Avoid: long story openers that need more than 140 chars to land

### Thread
Hook (tweet 1) must earn the click to "read more" AND signal there's a sequence worth reading.
- Add "🧵" or "Here's how:" or "A thread:" to signal continuation
- Bookmark prompt ("Save this") can be in tweet 1 or 2
- Prefer: curiosity gap, how-to promise, number-led

### Long Post
Opening paragraph functions as the hook. Must be a compelling standalone read.
- Prefer: story opener, bold claim, curiosity gap
- The opening paragraph is the only thing readers see before clicking "Show more"

### Image Post
Hook + image are a unit. Hook should add POV, not describe the image.
- Prefer: bold claim that the image proves, story opener that the image illustrates
- Keep to ≤140 chars so it reads above the fold before the image loads

### Video Post
First line = visual promise. Reader knows what they're about to see.
- "Watch me [do X] in [Y minutes]" is proven
- Prefer: how-to promise, bold claim
