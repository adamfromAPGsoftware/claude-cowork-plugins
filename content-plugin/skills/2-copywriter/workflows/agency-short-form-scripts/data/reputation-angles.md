## Hot Take Templates

Pre-seeded opinion angles tied to Adam's actual POV. Each has a trigger claim, the underlying belief, and the credibility tie-in.

### Take 01 — SaaS Pricing is Collapsing

**Hook:** "SaaS pricing is collapsing live and most SME owners haven't noticed yet."
**Underlying POV:** Per-user pricing was the 2015–2020 model. The category is bifurcating: all-in-one flat-fee platforms vs enterprise behemoths. Mid-market SaaS is getting squeezed out. SMEs who locked in per-user tools are overpaying for platforms they'll be forced to migrate off anyway.
**Evidence to pull:** HubSpot share price, Salesforce growth slowdown, category consolidation stats.
**Credibility tie:** "I've seen this shift from 300+ audits — the middle is disappearing."

---

### Take 02 — Software Projects Fail Before Code is Written

**Hook:** "The reason 90% of software projects fail isn't the code — it's what happens before the first line is written."
**Underlying POV:** Spec debt, misaligned stakeholders, and skipped discovery phases cause project failure. Most agencies skip the audit because it's harder to sell than the build. Adam leads with it.
**Evidence to pull:** Project failure rate stats (McKinsey, Standish Chaos Report).
**Credibility tie:** "I've been brought in to rescue failed projects more times than I care to count."

---

### Take 03 — AI Won't Save You With Scattered Data

**Hook:** "Everyone is telling SMEs to 'start using AI.' Nobody's telling them the actual prerequisite."
**Underlying POV:** AI operates on data. If your business data is spread across 12 disconnected SaaS tools, AI can't touch 90% of it. The AI conversation is 3 steps ahead of where most Australian SMEs actually are.
**Evidence to pull:** Australian SME tech adoption lag stats, AI readiness surveys.
**Credibility tie:** "The first thing I do in every audit is map the data — and it's almost always a mess."

## Voice Guidance — Reputation Scripts vs BOFU Scripts

| Dimension | BOFU Script | Reputation Script |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Primary goal** | Drive CTA click / keyword comment | Build authority, trust, recognisability |
| **Opening move** | Problem interrupt or pain reveal | Strong opinion, surprising stat, or contrarian claim |
| **Body structure** | Pain → consequence → bridge → CTA | Claim → evidence → implication → (optional) perspective prompt |
| **CTA** | Hard keyword (AUDIT / WASTE / AI) | None required. Soft: "Has this been your experience?" or "Drop YES if you agree." |
| **Tone** | Business operator who's been in their shoes | Confident industry voice — informed, direct, sometimes contrarian |
| **Mention of offer?** | Yes — always bridges to audit | No — never pitch. Authority earns the trust that BOFU scripts convert |
| **Numbers** | Client metrics, savings, project counts | Industry stats, market data, credibility anchors |
| **Danger zone** | Being too generic or feature-listing | Coming across as a lecture or a soft pitch in disguise |
| **Emotional target** | Fear + frustration (loss aversion) | Respect + curiosity (authority recognition + learning signal) |

## Reputation CTA Options

| Option | When to Use |
|--------|------------|
| No CTA — end on the point | Default for strong takes that land without prompting |
| "Has this been your experience?" | When the take is relatable and invites business owners to self-identify |
| "Drop YES if you agree." | For takes with broad consensus (tech lag, project failure) |
| "What's the most expensive tool in your stack that you'd kill if you could?" | Engagement bait — only for SaaS/pricing takes |
| "Curious what you've seen — let me know in the comments." | Conversational close for nuanced topics |

**NEVER use a keyword CTA (AUDIT / WASTE / AI / RESULTS) on a Reputation script.** That collapses the brand-building value and turns it into a BOFU script.
