# Hook Patterns

## Hook Archetypes

### 1. Contrarian Opener
Opens with a bold statement that challenges conventional wisdom.
```
"Most developers are getting AI coding completely wrong"
"Stop building AI agents the way everyone tells you to"
```
**Best for:** Text, Image | **ICP:** Builders primarily

### 2. Dollar-Amount Alarm
Leads with a specific financial figure that creates urgency.
```
"The $60,000 wake-up call most companies are ignoring"
"I saved $4,200/month by automating one workflow"
```
**Best for:** Text, Image, Carousel | **ICP:** SMEs primarily

### 3. Authority Stat
Cites a credible source + compelling statistic.
```
"MIT study confirms: 95% of AI investments fail to achieve ROI"
"LinkedIn data shows carousels get 1.6x the reach of text posts"
```
**Best for:** All formats | **ICP:** Both

### 4. Income + Achievement
Personal result that demonstrates expertise through outcomes.
```
"I made $100K+ selling AI on Upwork at 23 years old"
"Built 47 AI agents in 6 months — here's what actually worked"
```
**Best for:** Text, Video | **ICP:** Builders primarily

### 5. Value Anchor Gap
Establishes high value then reveals accessibility.
```
"I got paid over $3,000 for the raw version of this course"
"Companies pay $500/hr for this consulting — here it is free"
```
**Best for:** Carousel, Text | **ICP:** Both

### 6. Curiosity Gap Question
Opens with a question that creates an irresistible knowledge gap.
```
"What do the top 1% of AI consultants know that you don't?"
"Why did 3 enterprise clients fire their dev teams last month?"
```
**Best for:** All formats | **ICP:** Both

### 7. Pattern Interrupt
Breaks expected LinkedIn patterns with unexpected formatting or content.
```
"I deleted 90% of my tech stack yesterday."
"Here's a confession most agency owners won't make:"
```
**Best for:** Text, Image | **ICP:** Both

### 8. Social Proof + Number
Leads with a concrete number of people helped or outcomes achieved to establish authority instantly.
```
"I've helped over 2,000 people get their first AI automation client"
"Built AI systems for 60+ businesses — here's the pattern they all share"
```
**Best for:** Image, Text | **ICP:** Both
**Performance reference:** 1,730 total engagement on top example. Concrete numbers outperform vague claims every time.

### 9. News-jacking
Opens by citing a recent announcement or event from a recognisable brand, then pivots to an original take.
```
"YC just announced they're looking for AI-native agencies"
"OpenAI just dropped their new pricing — here's what it actually means"
```
**Best for:** Text, Image | **ICP:** Both
**Performance reference:** 803 total engagement. Higher share count than pure CTA posts — thought-leadership angle is more redistributable.

### 10. Binary Framework
Splits an industry or audience into two distinct paths and forces the reader to self-identify.
```
"Agencies split into two businesses: A) labor B) leverage — only one survives"
"There are two types of AI builders: tool users and system thinkers"
```
**Best for:** Carousel, Text | **ICP:** Both
**Performance reference:** 803 total engagement. Binary framing drives dual-audience engagement — each camp comments to defend their position.

## Format-Specific Hook Guidance

### Text Hooks
- Must carry full weight — no visual to support
- Hook IS the first 1-2 lines visible before "see more"
- Contrarian and pattern interrupt work best

### Image Hooks
- Hook and image must amplify each other
- Image creates the scroll-stop, hook creates the curiosity
- Dollar-amount and authority stat pair well with bold visuals

### Carousel Hooks
- Must create a curiosity gap the swipe mechanic rewards
- Promise a sequence, breakdown, or step-by-step
- Value anchor gap and authority stat work well
- Title slide hook complements but doesn't duplicate post text hook

### Video Hooks
- Must promise something visual — a demo, result, or process
- Income + achievement pairs well with video proof
- Hook should explain WHY the video is worth watching

## Hook Quality Rules

- Stop the scroll in under 2 seconds of reading
- Be specific (numbers, outcomes, timeframes) — not generic
- Target at least one ICP (builder or SME)
- NOT duplicate hooks already used (check derivative tracking)
- Create a curiosity gap or pattern interrupt
