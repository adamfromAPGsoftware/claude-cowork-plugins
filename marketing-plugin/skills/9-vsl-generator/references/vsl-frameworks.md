# VSL Script Frameworks

Reference frameworks for Video Sales Letter script generation. Each framework defines a section structure, timing guidelines, and recommended motion graphic types.

---

## Hormozi VSL Structure

Derived from the proven VSL (`content/standalone/2026-03-08-operational-audit-vsl/vsl-script.md`). Best for high-ticket B2B offers with quantifiable ROI.

| # | Section | Duration | Format | Speaker % | Graphic % | Persuasion Technique |
|---|---------|----------|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Hook | 15-20s | To camera | 100% | 0% | Pattern interrupt — bold claim, no intro/logo, mid-thought start |
| 2 | Agitate | 25-35s | Mixed | 50% | 50% | Cost amplification — make waste/pain visible with specific numbers |
| 3 | Prove You Understand | 25-35s | To camera | 90% | 10% | Real quotes from target audience — "I keep hearing..." |
| 4 | Social Proof | 40-50s | Mixed | 60% | 40% | Case study + credentials grid — transformation story |
| 5 | Offer / Value Stack | 35-45s | Mixed | 30% | 70% | Hormozi stack — list deliverables with individual values, total vs price |
| 6 | CTA / Guarantee | 35-45s | To camera | 80% | 20% | Risk reversal + urgency framing + qualification criteria |

**Total runtime:** 3:30-4:30
**Visual split:** ~65% speaker / ~35% graphics
**MG types:** waste-calculator, social-proof-grid, value-stack, guarantee-badge
**Tone:** Australian, direct, conversational. Not teleprompter-stiff.

### Section Details

**Hook:** Start mid-thought. No "hey guys", no logo, no intro card. First words ARE the hook. Lead with the biggest claim or most surprising data point. Format: full-frame speaker, direct eye contact.

**Agitate:** Make the cost vivid with specific numbers (daily, weekly, annual). "Death by a thousand cuts" pattern — list 3-5 specific waste categories. Cut to graphic showing the waste data visually. Formula: `Team Size × Hours × Rate × 52 = Annual Waste`.

**Prove You Understand:** Use real (anonymised) quotes from discovery calls with industry labels. Each quote: verbatim quote + industry + revenue + staff count. This section builds credibility by showing you've been in the room with people exactly like the viewer.

**Social Proof:** Tell ONE transformation story in detail (before → intervention → after with specific numbers). Then credentials rapid-fire: project count, success rate, community size, certifications, platform badges. Cut to credentials grid graphic.

**Offer / Value Stack:** "It's not a full build. It's not a 6-month project." Frame the offer simply first. Then Hormozi-style stack: list each deliverable with an individual dollar value, show total value struck through, reveal actual price. If proceeding to next phase, mention credit-back.

**CTA / Guarantee:** Money-back guarantee with specific threshold. "You keep every deliverable." No lock-in, no vendor dependency. Urgency: "The operational cost of waiting already pays for this." Qualification criteria (employee count, revenue threshold). Clear CTA: "Book a free discovery call. Link's right below this video."

## Mosing VSL Structure

Best for solution-aware audiences who need to understand the mechanism behind the offer.

| # | Section | Duration | Format | Speaker % | Graphic % | Persuasion Technique |
|---|---------|----------|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Cold Open | 10-15s | To camera | 100% | 0% | Provocative question or counterintuitive statement |
| 2 | The Problem | 25-35s | Mixed | 60% | 40% | Amplify with data and consequences |
| 3 | The Discovery | 35-50s | To camera | 80% | 20% | Story of how the solution was found |
| 4 | The Mechanism | 35-50s | Mixed | 50% | 50% | Explain WHY it works — not just what |
| 5 | Proof | 25-35s | Mixed | 40% | 60% | Results, testimonials, data |
| 6 | The Offer | 25-35s | Mixed | 30% | 70% | What they get, presented simply |
| 7 | Risk Reversal | 10-15s | To camera | 100% | 0% | Guarantee |
| 8 | Close | 10-15s | To camera | 80% | 20% | Single clear CTA |

**Total runtime:** 3:30-4:30
**Visual split:** ~60% speaker / ~40% graphics
**MG types:** data-comparison, mechanism-diagram, proof-grid, value-stack, cta-banner
**Tone:** Storytelling, building revelation momentum

## PAS-Long (Extended PAS for VSL)

Best for problem-aware audiences. Simplest structure, most direct.

| # | Section | Duration | Format | Speaker % | Graphic % | Persuasion Technique |
|---|---------|----------|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Pain | 25-35s | Mixed | 70% | 30% | Extended pain exploration with data |
| 2 | Agitate | 35-50s | Mixed | 50% | 50% | Consequences of inaction, cost of delay |
| 3 | Social Proof Bridge | 25-35s | Mixed | 60% | 40% | Others had this problem — here's what happened |
| 4 | Solution | 35-50s | Mixed | 50% | 50% | Detailed solution reveal with mechanism |
| 5 | Offer | 25-35s | Mixed | 30% | 70% | Value stack |
| 6 | Close | 25-35s | To camera | 80% | 20% | CTA with guarantee |

**Total runtime:** 3:00-4:00
**Visual split:** ~55% speaker / ~45% graphics
**MG types:** waste-calculator, before-after, value-stack, guarantee-badge, cta-banner

## Hybrid Framework

Mix sections from any framework. Must specify which sections come from which framework. Useful when the offer doesn't fit cleanly into one structure.

Rules:
- Hook/opening must always come first
- CTA/close must always come last
- Value stack and guarantee should be adjacent (stack → guarantee → CTA)
- Maintain a single narrative thread — don't jump between unrelated frameworks mid-video

## Timing Guidelines

- **Speaking pace:** ~150-180 words/minute (Australian conversational, fast and energetic)
- **Graphic hold:** 2-6 seconds per graphic (VSL graphics are rapid proof flashes)
- **Speaker max hold:** 8 seconds without an overlay (text breakout, icon, or graphic)
- **Layout changes:** 20-25 per minute throughout (energy never drops)
- **Total MGs:** 4-8 full-screen graphics for a standard 3-4 minute VSL
- **B-roll:** 2-4 segments, each 2-4 seconds, always B&W with text overlay

## Design Language

Match the proven VSL and landing page:

- **Background (graphics):** Dark grey/black `#0A0A0A` to `#1A1A1A`
- **Accent (positive):** Neon Green `#72E032`
- **Accent (negative):** Red `#EF4444`
- **Text:** White `#FFFFFF`, grey secondary `#9ca3af`
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans). Headlines bold, body regular.
- **Style:** Clean, data-driven, minimal. No stock photos in graphics. Dark mode aesthetic.
- **Logo:** {YOUR_COMPANY} logo (green gear) used as visual motif
