### Typography Direction

- **Headlines:** Bold, clean, high contrast. Sans-serif feel.
- **Body:** Readable, professional. Avoid decorative or script fonts.
- **Data/Numbers:** Monospace or tabular-aligned when showing metrics.

### Visual Style

- **Photography:** Natural lighting, warm tones, real environments (not stock photo studios)
- **People:** Diverse, professional but not corporate. Australian business context.
- **Tech elements:** Clean dashboards, process flows, automation visuals — never generic circuit boards or robot imagery
- **Composition:** Asymmetric balance, strong focal point, generous negative space

### Logo Usage

- Logo placement: Top-left corner or bottom-right corner
- Minimum clear space: Equal to the height of the "A" in {YOUR_COMPANY}
- Never stretch, rotate, or apply effects to the logo
- Do not place logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds

## What NOT to Do

- No generic "AI brain" or "neural network" imagery — overused and meaningless
- No blue-tinted everything — use the brand palette, not "tech blue" cliches
- No stock photo handshakes, pointing at whiteboards, or "diverse team looking at laptop"
- No competitor colours (avoid red/orange tech startup palettes)
- No cluttered designs — if it feels busy, it is busy
- No dark/dystopian AI imagery — our message is empowerment, not replacement
- No clip art, low-resolution imagery, or obvious AI artifacts (extra fingers, garbled text)
- No developer jargon in ad copy (API, webhook, pipeline) — save for technical buyers

## Ad-Specific Rules

### Meta Ads
- **Primary text:** 125 chars recommended, 250 max. Lead with value, not features.
- **Headlines:** 40 chars max. Direct, benefit-focused.
- **CTA buttons:** "Learn More" for awareness, "Get Started" for conversion, "Book Now" for appointments
- **Image text:** Keep text overlay minimal (Meta penalises text-heavy images). Max 20% of image area.
- **Video:** First 3 seconds must hook. No slow intros. Sound-off friendly (subtitles/visuals carry the message).

## Veo 3 Character Anchors (for AI-Generated Video Ads)

These character descriptions are used by the Creative Generator when building Veo 3 video prompts. They are NOT real people — they are AI-generated characters with consistent traits. The character anchor is defined ONCE per video and reused across ALL scenes to maintain visual consistency.

### Agency Founder Character

Use for: authority angles, proof stacks, founder-led storytelling, product walkthroughs.

```
Male, mid-30s, Australian. Athletic-lean build, 180cm. Short dark brown hair, clean fade sides, 
styled loosely on top. Light stubble (3-day growth), no full beard. Fair skin with slight tan. 
Clothing: fitted navy crew-neck t-shirt, quality fabric (not baggy). Silver watch, left wrist. 
No visible tattoos. Posture: upright, shoulders back, relaxed confidence — not stiff. 
Expression baseline: calm authority with slight warmth in the eyes. Slight asymmetric smile 
when making a point. Energy: measured, credible — like explaining something complex to a 
smart friend over coffee. Accent direction: Australian, conversational, not formal.
```

### Agency PM Character

Use for: empowerment angles, "junior PM delivers senior scopes" stories, team scaling.

```
Female, late-20s, Australian. Medium build, 170cm. Shoulder-length dark hair, straight, 
sometimes tucked behind one ear. No glasses. Medium-warm skin tone. Clothing: smart casual — 
light grey blazer over a white crew-neck, dark jeans. Small stud earrings. Posture: slightly 
forward-leaning when engaged, attentive. Expression baseline: focused competence with 
occasional bright smile when showing results. Energy: capable, rising confidence — someone 
growing into authority. Accent direction: Australian, clear, professional.
```

### Expression Rules for Ad Video

| Allowed | Forbidden |
|---------|-----------|
| Confident, direct eye contact | Shocked, jaw-drop |
| Thoughtful (slight head tilt) | Wide-eyed surprise |
| Slight smile (asymmetric) | Exaggerated excitement |
| Curious (eyebrow micro-raise) | Unhappy, frowning |
| Calm authority | Overly serious/stern |
| Genuine warmth | Fake/forced smile |

**Movement rules:** Characters must be IN MOTION — walking, sitting down, picking up a coffee, gesturing at a screen, shifting weight between feet. Static talking heads look AI-generated. Movement is the primary realism tool for Veo 3.

## How to Apply in Creative Generation

When building image/video prompts:
1. Reference specific hex colours for backgrounds, accents, and CTAs
2. Describe the visual tone (professional but approachable, warm, real)
3. Include composition guidance (where to leave text space)
4. Mention what to avoid (see "What NOT to Do" above)
5. For people shots: specify "Australian professional" context, natural setting
6. For stats/data angles: pull from the ROI Framework section
7. For social proof angles: reference specific case studies with real numbers
8. For comparison angles: use the Competitive Differentiators table
9. Always use ICP pain/aspiration language — never developer jargon
